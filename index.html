How to play Truth or Dare

Players take turns choosing "truth" — and honestly answering a question — or "dare" — and completing a small challenge. Go around a circle, spin a bottle to pick who's next, or just call on someone. The only real rule is that everyone plays fair: answer truthfully if you pick truth, and actually do the dare if you pick dare. Every prompt in this generator is kept clean and good-natured, so it works for classrooms, sleepovers, family game night, and party warm-ups.

Pick the right group

  • Party — lighthearted truths and silly dares for friends and mixed groups.
  • Kids — wholesome, giggly prompts safe for younger players.
  • Couples — sweet, romantic questions and cute dares for date night.

Ways to keep it fun

  • Set a "chicken" rule: skip a prompt once per game, but you owe the group an extra dare later.
  • Use the Truth only or Dare only setting when the group is in the mood for one or the other.
  • Generate a batch of ten before you start so there's no waiting between turns.

About these prompts

All prompts are written to be family-friendly and are generated in your browser — nothing is stored and nothing is inappropriate. If a dare ever doesn't fit your space, just tap again for a new one.
